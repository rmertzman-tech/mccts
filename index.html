Purpose

The student’s reasoning process becomes the object of study

EECSL examines public cases. The Capability Lab examines simulations and institutional systems. This studio examines the student's own movement from first moral recognition to public, qualified, rebuttable, repair-oriented reasoning.

Core insight:

The warrant is where OS1 experience begins to become OS2 reasoning. It is also where a student's PRF becomes visible in argument form.

Where This Tool Fits

Four instruments with different jobs

This panel prevents the tool ecosystem from becoming blurred. MCCTS should not become the Navigator, EECSL, or Capability Lab. Its special task is the warrant-centered OS1 → OS2 bridge.

Navigator

Private, longitudinal reflection. Students develop Field Scans, PRF awareness, and personal meaning over time.

Capability Lab

Public simulation environment. Students examine emergence, coordination, warning, institutional pressure, and repair.

EECSL

Public empirical case library. Students examine evidence-labeled institutional cases and cross-case patterns.

MCCTS

Metacognitive bridge. Students examine their own warrants and translate felt certainty into public reasoning.

Dual OS1 Perception Check

Two honest OS1 reactions can collide

The teaching point is not that OS1 is bad. OS1 is morally important because it shows what feels salient from within a lived PRF. But OS1 certainty is not yet public reasoning.

Boisjoly / Engineering Safety PRF

felt certainty “This is wrong. We know enough not to launch.”

Notices: cold O-ring risk, expert warning, crew vulnerability, catastrophic asymmetry.

May hide: how institutional decision rules shift the burden of proof onto those trying to stop action.

Mulloy / Launch Management PRF

felt certainty “We cannot stop a national launch schedule without decisive proof.”

Notices: prior successful launches, operational pressure, burden of proof, institutional credibility.

May hide: catastrophic asymmetry, expert-domain warning, and the suppression gap.

Warrant Range Display

Challenger can be warranted through several ethical lenses

Clarifying the warrant gap:

The Challenger demo uses one possible warrant. The cards below show how different ethical lenses can warrant the same claim in different ways. Students should ask: Which warrant did my PRF reach for first, and which warrant did I not naturally see?

Epistemic / Suppression-Gap Warrant

When institutional authority systematically overrides domain expertise in safety-critical decisions, the institutional architecture bears responsibility for predictable outcomes.

knowledge · voice · authority

Kantian / Persons-as-Ends Warrant

Persons who may die from a decision cannot be treated merely as schedule variables, public-relations burdens, or operational constraints.

dignity · duty · ends

Care Ethics Warrant

Those with the most at stake and the least institutional voice deserve proportional moral attention, responsiveness, and protection.

vulnerability · response · dependency

Agency Loop

The two-sided model of agency

One side is lived phenomenal certitude: the world shows up as wrong, urgent, unfair, admirable, dangerous, or meaningful. The other side is uncertainty-aware modeling: claims must be public, evidence-sensitive, qualified, rebuttable, and repair-oriented.
