Purpose

From felt certainty to public reasoning

This studio helps students recognize the authority and limits of immediate moral experience. A first reaction may reveal something important, but it is not yet a public argument. The task is to translate that reaction into claims, evidence, warrants, qualifications, objections, and repair-oriented proposals that others can examine.

Core teaching claim:

Students do not abandon moral emotion or lived experience. They learn to move from OS1 felt certitude into OS2 shared reasoning without pretending that uncertainty means “nothing can be known.”

Design Specification

What this site is for

The studio is a standalone public reasoning layer. It does not replace EECSL, the Capability Lab, or the private Navigator. It connects them by giving students a disciplined method for translating observations, reactions, and case interpretations into accountable reasoning.

Public-site boundary:

This page should not collect private student disclosures. Students may use public cases, fictional examples, or instructor-provided scenarios. Private reflection belongs in the Navigator, a private notebook, or an instructor-approved LMS space.

Student Start

How to use this studio

  1. Start with a public case, class scenario, simulation, or instructor-provided question.
  2. Name your first reaction without treating it as the final argument.
  3. Use the Coherence Bridge to notice what kind of reasoning support may help right now.
  4. Use the OS1 → OS2 section to translate the reaction into a public claim.
  5. Use Toulmin structure to strengthen the claim.
  6. Use the framework map to test whether another ethical lens reveals something you missed.
  7. End with one repair-oriented next step.
Expected public output:

A short reasoning statement that names a claim, evidence, warrant, qualifier, possible objection, and repair proposal.

OS1: I feel strongly that ______ because ______.

OS2: Publicly, I can support the claim that ______ with the evidence ______. The warrant connecting my evidence to my claim is ______. I should qualify the claim by admitting ______. A repair-oriented next step would be ______.

Toulmin Practice

Argument-building structure

For Phase Q, this remains a stable scaffold. Phase S will add full interactive Toulmin fields and copyable argument cards.

Toulmin elementGuiding question
ClaimWhat am I asking others to accept?
GroundsWhat evidence, case facts, or observations support the claim?
WarrantWhy do these grounds support this claim?
BackingWhat theory, principle, source, or framework supports the warrant?
QualifierHow strong is the claim, and under what limits?
RebuttalWhat would a reasonable critic ask or object?
Repair statementWhat constructive action follows if the argument is accepted?
