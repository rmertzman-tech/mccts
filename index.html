Purpose

From felt certainty to public reasoning

This studio helps students recognize the authority and limits of immediate moral experience. A first reaction may reveal something important, but it is not yet a public argument. The task is to translate that reaction into claims, evidence, warrants, qualifications, objections, and repair-oriented proposals that others can examine.

Core teaching claim:

Students do not abandon moral emotion or lived experience. They learn to move from OS1 felt certitude into OS2 shared reasoning without pretending that uncertainty means “nothing can be known.”

Phase Q.1 addition:

This build adds guided demo examples, standard arguments to analyze, and a Warrant Bridge showing why the warrant is the place where a student’s PRF becomes visible in argument form.

Warrant Bridge

Why the warrant matters

The claim shows what a student concludes. The grounds show what evidence the student notices. The warrant shows why that evidence is being treated as relevant. In EBB terms, the warrant is where the student’s PRF becomes visible in argument form.

Teaching move:

Ask: “What must be true for these grounds to support this claim?” That question reveals assumptions, values, ontologies, duties, and models that are often hidden inside OS1 certainty.

Agency Loop Explainer

The two-sided agency model

The Studio is built around a dual structure of agency. One side is lived phenomenal certitude: the world shows up as wrong, urgent, unfair, admirable, dangerous, or meaningful. The other side is uncertainty-aware modeling: claims must be public, evidence-sensitive, qualified, rebuttable, and repair-oriented.

Critical-thinking loop:

Experience shapes perception. Perception triggers OS1 recognition. Metacognition slows the reaction. Toulmin structure converts the reaction into OS2 reasoning. New evidence and criticism revise the PRF, changing what the agent notices next time.
