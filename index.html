Purpose

The student’s reasoning process becomes the object of study

EECSL examines public cases. The Capability Lab examines simulations and institutional systems. This studio examines the student’s own movement from first moral recognition to public, qualified, rebuttable, repair-oriented reasoning.

Core insight:

The warrant is where OS1 experience begins to become OS2 reasoning. It is also where a student’s PRF becomes visible in argument form.

Warrant Bridge

Why the warrant matters

The claim shows what a student concludes. The grounds show what evidence the student notices. The warrant shows why that evidence is being treated as relevant.

Deeper philosopher modeling:

MCCTS is the accessible practice environment. For deeper exploration of how specific philosophical traditions structure warrants, use the IEIA / Philosophical Traditions tool in the Case Handoff Library.

QuestionWhat it reveals
What must be true for these grounds to support this claim?The hidden warrant.
What does the warrant make visible?The value, duty, harm, or model being prioritized.
What does the warrant hide?The alternate PRF, framework, voice, or assumption being backgrounded.
What should change in future perception?The PRF revision note.

Agency Loop

The two-sided model of agency

One side is lived phenomenal certitude: the world shows up as wrong, urgent, unfair, admirable, dangerous, or meaningful. The other side is uncertainty-aware modeling: claims must be public, evidence-sensitive, qualified, rebuttable, and repair-oriented.
